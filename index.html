Why Hydration Matters

Water is your
body's critical fuel.

Most people don't drink nearly enough. The consequences are real, common, and entirely avoidable.

🧠

Brainpower drops fast

Even mild dehydration (just 1–2% body weight) can significantly reduce concentration, memory, and cognitive performance.

⚡

Energy follows hydration

Fatigue is one of the first signals of dehydration. Staying topped up keeps cells energised and mood stable all day.

🩺

Long-term health impact

Chronic dehydration is linked to kidney stones, UTIs, digestive problems, and increased cardiovascular risk.

💪

Real performance gains

Athletes who track hydration see measurable gains in endurance, strength, and recovery speed compared to those who don't.
